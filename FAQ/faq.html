Questions?

By default Ration chooses to display non-targeted advertisements, and WILL NOT use a personal advertising profile from your device for ads unless you choose “Enable Targeted Advertising” in your settings.


Ration also collects anonymous user data to aid its internal functionality. This includes but is not limited to:

  • The frequency/time/quantity in which items/Custom Meals are bought or expire
  • Carbon footprint
  • Location for scanning local pantries

If at any point you feel uncomfortable with this collection, you can opt out at any time in the settings by choosing “Opt Out of Functional Data Collection” .

You will need to log an item’s status in the app.

The Expiration Calculator is meant to calculate the approximate time in which complex, home cooked meals might go bad. It works primarily by referencing its default database for home cooked items.

For a potentially better estimate, you can also receive the average time it took for other Ration users’ Custom Meals to go bad or have the app compare the times of the other users who had similar ingredients (ingredients from the same food brands).

You can scan your item through barcode or image or manually type in your food item on the app.

When you finish making your pantry, click the “Share” button to generate a link or QR code for other Ration users to access your pantry. Once you share a pantry, you become the “Admin” that can drop other users and determine who has privileges to edit.

Our database is made up of two components, food item data and food expiry data.

Food Item data comes from Ration users and our APIs.

Expiry data for most foods comes from the United States Food Safety and Inspection Service directly from the United States' Food and Safety Inspection Service . Data for Custom Meals not found in the FSIS database may also come from Ration users with similar meals.
